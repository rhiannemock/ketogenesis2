About Keto

The ketogenic diet, or keto for short, is a low-carb, high-fat diet that has gained popularity for its potential health benefits, including weight loss and improved energy levels. By drastically reducing carbohydrate intake and replacing it with fat, the body enters a metabolic state known as ketosis, where it becomes efficient at burning fat for energy.

The keto diet can have numerous health benefits, weight loss, blood sugar stabilisation, IBS and bloating improvement, chronic fatigue, brain fog and mental cognition, menopause symptoms, and can even improve epilepsy.

In this section, we will explore the fundamentals of the ketogenic diet, its benefits, and how to get started on your keto journey.

Meat

Meat is a staple of the ketogenic diet. It is a great source of protein and healthy fats, which are essential for maintaining ketosis. When choosing meat, it is best to opt for unprocessed, high-quality options that are free from added sugars and preservatives.

It is important to remember that a keto diet is a low carbohydrate, moderate protein and high fat diet. Too much meat can mean too much protein, so tracking your daily macronutrients is essential to stay in ketosis.

Excess protein can be converted to glucose in a process called gluconeogenesis, which will hinder the body’s ability to produce ketone bodies and achieve the desired results.

Here is a list of the most commonly consumed meats:

  • Chicken
  • Turkey breast
  • Beef
  • Steak
  • Pork Loin
  • Liver
  • Bacon

Seafood

Both fish and seafood are excellent sources of essential fatty acids (omega-3), high-quality protein and vitamin D. Due to their low carbohydrate content, they are ideal for consumption in a ketogenic diet.

Here are some of the most popular fish and seafood that can be consumed on a ketogenic diet:

  • Salmon
  • Mackerel
  • Tuna
  • Herring
  • Cod
  • Shrimp
  • Crabs
  • Lobsters
  • Mussels

Healthy fats

Healthy fats are the cornerstone of the ketogenic diet. They provide the body with a source of energy and help to maintain ketosis. It is important to choose healthy fats that are rich in omega-3 and omega-6 fatty acids, as well as monounsaturated and polyunsaturated fats.

Healthy fats are essential for maintaining a state of ketosis, which is the goal of the ketogenic diet. The body uses fat as its primary source of energy, and healthy fats provide the necessary nutrients to support this process.

Here are some of the best sources of healthy fats that can be consumed on a ketogenic diet:

Saturated fats:

These fats are solid at room temperature and are found in animal products such as meat, eggs, and dairy. They are also found in coconut oil and palm oil.

Examples of saturated fats include:

red meat, eggs, butter, coconut oil, MCT oil

Monounsaturated fats:

These fats are usually liquid at room temperature and are found in olive oil, avocado oil, and nuts. They are known to be heart-healthy and can help lower bad cholesterol levels.

Examples of monounsaturated fats include:

lard, bacon, fatty fish, olive oil, avocado oil, nuts, chia seeds

Polyunsaturated fats:

These fats are also liquid at room temperature and are found in fish, nuts, and seeds. They are known to be anti-inflammatory and can help improve heart health.

Examples of polyunsaturated fats include:

fish such as salmon or mackerel, nuts, avocado, flaxseed oil, virgin olive oil extra virgin olive oil. Plus dont forget that not all polyunsaturated fats are healthy.

Perfect keto foods

Here are some of the best keto foods that you can include in your diet:

Eggs

Eggs are a great source of protein and healthy fats, making them an ideal food for the ketogenic diet. They are also low in carbohydrates, which makes them a perfect choice for those looking to maintain ketosis.

Eggs are versatile and can be prepared in many different ways, such as scrambled, hard-boiled, sunny side-up, or poached. They can be eaten on their own or added to salads, omelets, and other dishes.

When choosing eggs, it is best to opt for pastured organic eggs, which are higher in omega-3 fatty acids and other nutrients. These eggs come from chickens that are raised on a natural diet and have access to the outdoors, which results in healthier eggs.

Dairy

Dairy products are a great source of healthy fats and protein, making them an ideal food for the ketogenic diet. They are also low in carbohydrates, which makes them a perfect choice for those looking to maintain ketosis.

When choosing dairy products, it is best to opt for full-fat options that are free from added sugars and preservatives. Full-fat dairy products are higher in healthy fats and lower in carbohydrates, making them a better choice for the ketogenic diet.

Here are some of the best dairy products that can be included in a ketogenic diet:

What foods should you avoid

When following a ketogenic diet, it is important to avoid foods that are high in carbohydrates and sugars. These foods can hinder the body’s ability to enter and maintain ketosis, which is the goal of the ketogenic diet.

Here are some of the foods that should be avoided on a ketogenic diet:

  • Grains (such as wheat, rice, and corn)
  • Starchy vegetables (such as potatoes, sweet potatoes, and corn)
  • Legumes (such as beans, lentils, and peas)
  • Fruits (except for low-carb options such as berries and avocados)
  • Sugary foods and drinks (such as candy, soda, and fruit juice)
  • Processed foods (such as chips, crackers, and baked goods)
  • High-carb snacks (such as popcorn, pretzels, and granola bars)
  • High-carb condiments (such as ketchup, barbecue sauce, and salad dressings)
  • High-carb dairy products (such as milk and yogurt)
  • High-carb alcoholic beverages (such as beer and sweet wines)
  • High-carb sauces and marinades (such as teriyaki sauce and sweet and sour sauce)
  • High-carb breakfast cereals (such as oatmeal and granola)
  • High-carb baked goods (such as bread, muffins, and pastries)
  • High-carb snacks (such as chips, crackers, and popcorn)
  • High-carb desserts (such as cakes, cookies, and ice cream)
  • High-carb sauces and dressings (such as ketchup, barbecue sauce, and salad dressings)

Common Keto Questions and Answers

1. What is the Ketogenic Diet?

The ketogenic diet is a very low-carb, high-fat, and moderate-protein eating plan designed to shift your body into a metabolic state called ketosis, where it burns fat for fuel instead of carbohydrates.

2. What is Ketosis?

Ketosis is a metabolic state where your body, lacking sufficient glucose from carbohydrates, begins to break down fats into molecules called ketones, which it then uses as its primary energy source.

3. What are Macronutrients (Macros) in Keto?

Macros refer to the three main categories of nutrients your body needs: fats, proteins, and carbohydrates. On keto, the macro distribution is typically around 70-75% fat, 20-25% protein, and 5-10% carbohydrates.

4. How many carbs can I eat on Keto?

Typically, carbohydrate intake is restricted to 20-50 grams of net carbs per day (total carbs minus fibre).

5. What is "Net Carbs"?

Net carbs are calculated by subtracting the grams of fibre (and sometimes sugar alcohols) from the total grams of carbohydrates in a food. Fibre is generally not digested and thus doesn't raise blood sugar.

6. What foods are allowed on Keto?

Allowed foods include meat, fish, eggs, butter, healthy oils, avocados, nuts, seeds, cheese, and low-carb vegetables (like leafy greens, broccoli, cauliflower).

7. What foods should I avoid on Keto?

Foods to avoid include grains (bread, pasta, rice), sugar, most fruits (except small amounts of berries), starchy vegetables (potatoes, corn), legumes, and processed foods with added sugars.

8. What is the "Keto Flu"?

The keto flu is a collection of symptoms (like fatigue, headache, irritability, nausea) that some people experience during the initial adaptation phase as their body switches from burning carbs to burning fat.

9. How long does the "Keto Flu" last?

It typically lasts a few days to a week and can often be mitigated by increasing electrolyte intake (sodium, potassium, magnesium) and staying hydrated.

10. How long does it take to enter Ketosis?

It usually takes 2-7 days to enter a state of nutritional ketosis, depending on individual metabolism and strict adherence to carbohydrate restriction.

11. How can I tell if I'm in Ketosis?

You can measure ketone levels using urine strips, blood ketone meters, or breath analyzers. Physical signs can include increased energy, suppressed appetite, and "keto breath" (a metallic taste or fruity smell).

Benefits & Outcomes

12. Is the Ketogenic Diet good for weight loss?

Yes, many people find it effective for weight loss due to its appetite-suppressing effects, increased satiety from fat and protein, and the body's shift to burning stored fat.

13. Can Keto help with Type 2 Diabetes?

By drastically reducing carbohydrate intake, keto can significantly lower blood sugar levels and improve insulin sensitivity, making it potentially beneficial for managing (and sometimes reversing) Type 2 Diabetes, under medical supervision.

14. What are the main benefits of the Keto Diet?

Benefits can include weight loss, improved blood sugar control, increased mental clarity, sustained energy levels, and potential therapeutic effects for certain neurological conditions like epilepsy.

15. Are there any risks or side effects?

Potential side effects include the keto flu, nutrient deficiencies (if not well-planned), kidney stones, and in rare cases, more serious complications. Long-term effects are still being researched.

16. Do I need to count calories on Keto?

While the keto diet can naturally reduce calorie intake due to satiety, counting calories is often still necessary for consistent weight loss, especially as the body adapts.

Practical Considerations & Specifics

17. Can I drink alcohol on Keto?

Some low-carb alcoholic beverages (like dry wine or spirits with sugar-free mixers) can be consumed in moderation, but alcohol can slow down ketosis and has calories. Sugary drinks should be avoided.

18. What about fruits on Keto?

Most fruits are high in sugar and carbs and should be avoided. Small amounts of berries (like raspberries, blueberries, strawberries) can be consumed in moderation due to their lower net carb count.

19. Is Keto a sustainable long-term diet?

Sustainability varies by individual. Some people find it easy to maintain long-term, while others prefer to cycle in and out of ketosis. It requires strict adherence and careful planning.

20. Should I consult a doctor before starting Keto?

Absolutely. It is highly recommended to consult with a healthcare professional or a registered dietitian before starting the ketogenic diet, especially if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or are on medication.

21. What is a typical keto breakfast?

A common keto breakfast might include eggs cooked in butter or avocado oil with bacon, sausage, or avocado, and a side of low-carb vegetables like spinach.

22. Can vegetarians or vegans do Keto?

Yes, but it can be more challenging. They would rely heavily on healthy fats (avocado, nuts, seeds, olive oil), low-carb vegetables, and plant-based protein sources like tofu, tempeh, and certain protein powders.

Additional Keto FAQs

23. What are electrolytes and why are they important on Keto?

Electrolytes (sodium, potassium, magnesium) are minerals crucial for nerve and muscle function, and hydration. On keto, reduced carb intake can lead to increased water and electrolyte excretion, making supplementation important to prevent "keto flu" symptoms.

24. Can I eat dairy on Keto?

Yes, most full-fat dairy products like butter, heavy cream, and cheese are keto-friendly due to their high fat and low carb content. Avoid milk and low-fat dairy as they contain more lactose (sugar).

25. What role does fibre play in a Keto diet?

Fibre is important on keto for digestive health. It's a carbohydrate that isn't digested, so it doesn't affect blood sugar and is subtracted to calculate net carbs. Focus on fiber from low-carb vegetables and seeds.

26. What are the best snacks for Keto?

Good keto snacks include nuts (almonds, macadamias), seeds (chia, flax), cheese, olives, avocado, hard-boiled eggs, deli meats, and low-carb jerky.

27. How does Keto affect exercise performance?

Initially, exercise performance may decrease as the body adapts. Once fat-adapted, many people experience sustained energy for endurance activities, but high-intensity, short-burst activities might be impacted without readily available glucose.

28. What is exogenous ketosis?

Exogenous ketones are ketone supplements taken externally to raise blood ketone levels. They can help with keto-adaptation symptoms but don't automatically put you into a fat-burning state for weight loss like nutritional ketosis does.

29. Can I eat fast food on Keto?

It's challenging but possible with careful choices. Opt for bunless burgers, salads with high-fat dressings (without croutons or sugary toppings), or grilled chicken without breading. Always check nutritional information.

30. What's the difference between Keto and Atkins?

Both are low-carb diets. Atkins typically has phases that gradually reintroduce more carbs, while keto maintains a consistently very low carb intake to stay in ketosis. Keto is usually stricter on fat intake being high.

31. How does Keto affect cholesterol levels?

Keto often leads to an increase in HDL ("good") cholesterol and a decrease in triglycerides. Effects on LDL ("bad") cholesterol can vary; some see an increase, others a decrease or no change. Individual responses differ.

32. Are artificial sweeteners allowed on Keto?

Some artificial sweeteners like erythritol, stevia, monk fruit, and sucralose (in moderation) are generally allowed as they don't significantly impact blood sugar. Others, like maltitol, can have a greater impact.

33. How do I track my macros on Keto?

You can track macros using food tracking apps or by manually logging your food intake. This helps ensure you stay within your carb limit and hit your fat and protein targets.

34. What are some common mistakes beginners make on Keto?

Common mistakes include not eating enough fat, not consuming enough electrolytes, underestimating carb counts, not drinking enough water, and not giving the body enough time to adapt.

35. Can Keto cause bad breath?

Yes, "keto breath" is a common side effect, often described as a fruity or metallic smell. It's caused by the excretion of acetone (a type of ketone) through the breath and usually diminishes over time.

36. What is "carb cycling" on Keto?

Carb cycling involves alternating periods of low-carb/keto eating with days of higher carbohydrate intake. Some people use it to support athletic performance or make the diet more sustainable, but it can disrupt ketosis.

37. What are the best oils to use on Keto?

Healthy fats are crucial. Good choices include olive oil, avocado oil, coconut oil, MCT oil, and butter/ghee. Avoid highly processed vegetable oils like soybean and corn oil.

38. How does Keto impact sleep?

Initially, some people report sleep disturbances (insomnia) during adaptation. Once fat-adapted, many experience improved sleep quality, deeper sleep, and fewer nighttime awakenings.

39. Can children or teenagers do Keto?

The ketogenic diet for children is primarily used under strict medical supervision for therapeutic purposes (e.g., epilepsy). It is generally not recommended for healthy children or teenagers due to growth and nutritional needs without expert guidance.

40. What are some easy Keto meal prep ideas?

Meal prep ideas include preparing large batches of grilled chicken or salmon, roasted low-carb vegetables, making fat bombs, or cooking a big pot of chili (keto-friendly ingredients).

41. How does Keto affect my gut health?

The impact on gut health can vary. Some studies suggest it can shift the gut microbiome, while others emphasize the importance of diverse low-carb vegetables to provide fiber for beneficial gut bacteria.

42. What are hidden carbs and how do I spot them?

Hidden carbs are carbohydrates found in unexpected places, like sauces, dressings, processed meats, spices, and "sugar-free" products that might contain sugar alcohols or fillers. Always read labels carefully.
